About

Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan was a Pakistani musician and the greatest exponent of Qawwali — the devotional music of the Sufi Muslims. Born in 1948 in Faisalabad, he came from a family of musicians stretching back over 600 years.

He introduced Qawwali to the world stage, collaborating with Peter Gabriel and recording for Real World Records. His voice had a range and power that left audiences speechless — from tiny village shrines to Carnegie Hall.

He passed in 1997, but his music is eternal.
